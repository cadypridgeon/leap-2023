Probability and Statistics

Introduction

We are getting deeper into the world of statistics. We will be learning more about probability distributions as we discuss organizing and interpreting data for real-world scenarios.

Definitions

  • Probability Distribution: Probability Distribution is a table or equation that can represent the probability of the outcome of a statistical experiment.
  • Mean: The mean is the average value of a set of outcomes.

Example

There are five students in a class and they are taking an exam. The scores of the exams are 60, 70, 72, 85, and 90. What is the average score (or mean) of the grades?

Solution

The average score for the exam is 75.4. You get this by adding all the scores together 60 + 70 + 72 + 85 + 90 = 377. Then divide that value by the amount of students, 377 / 5 = 75.4.

Quiz Question

A gardener measures all her tomato plants. They are the following heights: 3ft, 4ft, 4.2 ft, 4.5ft, and 6ft. What is the average height of her tomato plants?

Correct!

The correct answer is 4.34 ft. You get this by adding the heights of all the plants together, 3 + 4 + 4.2 + 4.5 + 6 = 21.7. Then divide 21.7 by the amount of tomato plants, 21.7 / 5 = 4.34.
