Geometry

Congruent and Similar Figures

Definitions

  • Congruent: shapes that are exactly same shape and sizes
  • Similar figures: figures that have similar shapes, mirror images, or same shape but different sizes

Example

Consider two rectangles, P and Q, where the length of rectangle P is 6 cm and the width is 4 cm. Rectangle Q has a length of 8 cm and a width of 3 cm.

Solution

To determine if the rectangles are similar, we compare the ratios of their corresponding sides. The ratio of the length to width for rectangle P is 6:4, which simplifies to 3:2. For rectangle Q, the ratio is 8:3. Since the ratios are not equal, the rectangles are not similar. Congruent figures have both equal angles and equal side lengths.
