Probability and Statistics

Introduction

Today we are going to explore data representation and what values are important to note when analyzing data.

Definitions

  • Mean: The mean is the average value of a set of outcomes.
  • Median: The middle of a set of data.
  • Maximum: The maximum value in a set of data.
  • Minimim: The minimum value in a set of data.

Example

Say you are a teacher grading math papers and your students score the following: 52, 55, 60, 65, 76, 78, 83, 86, 92, and 99. If the average is above 80, your students will get a special treat. Will the class get a treat?

Solution

The students will not get a treat. To get the average you add all the values together 52 + 55 + 60 + 65 + 76 + 78 + 83 + 86 + 92 + 99 = 746. You take the value and divide it by the number of students, 746 / 10 = 74.6. 74.6 is below 80 so the students will have to study harder next time.

Quiz Question

Given the following data about rainfall in Chicago in the second week of October, identify the max and min. Rainfall: 4.5 in, 0 in, 0 in, 6 in, 2 in, 1 in, and 0.5 in.

Correct!

The correct answer is a max of 6 and a min of 0. 6 is the largest number in the given set and 0 is the smallest number in the given set.
