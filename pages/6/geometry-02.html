Volume of Rectangular Prisms

Besides area, volume is another important concept that helps introduce how space is measured when considering three-dimensional space. To introduce the concept of volume, we will focus on rectangular prisms. Rectangular prisms can be visualized by imagining stacking blocks to create a rectangular structure, and we need to know how much space those blocks occupy. To find the volume, you must multiply its length, width, and height.

Example

What is the volume of a rectangular prism with a length of 4 units, a width of 3 units, and a height of 2 units?

Solution

To solve for the volume, we must multiply the rectangular prism's length * width * height. By plugging in these values, we have the expression 4 * 3 * 2, which is equal to 24. This means our rectangular prism has a volume of 24 cubic units.

Quiz Question

Find the volume of a rectangular prism with a length of 8 units, a width of 6 units, and a height of 4 units.

Correct!

The correct answer is that the volume of the rectangular prism is 192 cubic units. To solve, we multiply its length, width, and height, leaving us with the expression 8 * 6 * 4. 8 * 6 = 48, and 48 * 4 is 192, meaning our rectangular prism has a volume of 192 cubic units.
