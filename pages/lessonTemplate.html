Ratio and Proportional Relationships

I. Ratios and Ratio Relations

Understand the concept of a ratio and use ratio language to describe a ratio relationship between two quantities.

Definitions

  • A ratio is a comparison between two or more quantities, often expressed as a fraction. It shows how many times one quantity is contained within another.
  • Ratio relationships involve comparing the quantities of different objects, numbers, or variables. They help us understand how one quantity is related to another in terms of size or amount.

Example

If you have 2 red marbles and 3 blue marbles, what is the ratio of red marbles to blue marbles?

Solution

The ratio of red marbles to blue marbles is 2:3.

Quiz Question

In a fruit basket, there are 3 apples and 2 oranges. What is the ratio of apples to oranges?

Correct!

To find the ratio of apples to oranges, we divide the number of apples by the number of oranges.
Ratio = Number of Apples:Number of Oranges = 12:8 = 3:2
This means that for every 3 apples, there are 2 oranges in the fruit basket. Therefore, the correct answer is 3:2.
